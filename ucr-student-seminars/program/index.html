TBD Multi-Fidelity Emulation for Cosmological Simulations
Ming-Feng Ho (University of California, Riverside)
We present the first implementation of multi-fidelity emulation in cosmology, where many low-resolution simulations are combined with a few high-resolution simulations to improve emulation accuracy. Next-generation telescopes (e.g., Roman space telescope) will probe the small scales with high accuracy. Thus, expensive numerical simulations running in a huge box with a high resolution are required to extract information from the incoming data, making the Bayesian parameter inference costly to perform. Multi-fidelity emulation uses many low-resolution simulations to learn the dependence of parameters while only using a few costly high-resolution simulations to calibrate the resolution. We verify our method on the matter power spectrum, showing that our emulation can achieve percent-level accuracy on average with only three high-fidelity simulations and outperform a single-fidelity emulator using 11 simulations. Our proposed emulator shows a new way to predict non-linear scales by fusing simulations from different fidelities, making the emulation development substantially more practical.
