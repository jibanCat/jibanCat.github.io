Research Interests

I am interested in intergalactic medium and numerical cosmology. I am also interested in using machine learning to model quasars emissions, the lights from supermassive black holes. I improved a Gaussian process (GP) based model (original author: Roman Garnett [Garnett(2017)]) to find damped Lyman alpha absorbers (DLAs) in the intergalactic medium with consideration of weak Lyman alpha absorptions [code] [Ho-Bird-Garnett (2020)]. I also use the GP model to find DLAs in SDSS DR16 [code] [Ho-Bird-Garnett (2021)] We also performed probabilistic inference on quasar redshifts using our Gaussian process based model. [code][Fauber et al. (2020)]. I am currently working on building multi-fidelity emulators on a cosmological simulation- MP-Gadget, see Ho-Bird-Shelton (2021).

Motivation

My research motivation comes from my enthusiasm for helping relieve people from labour-intensive works in academia. During my time pursuing higher education, I have seen countless people lose their trust in research. They were told devoting time to labour-intensive jobs was the most efficient way to produce research results for their labs. I have a strong belief that automated tools or better models can replace all repetitive works. Academia is a place for creative thinking. At least in my field, I want to help astronomers automate their works through combining physical models and machine learning. To systematically change things, I have to keep doing research on both astronomy data (e.g., Lyman alpha forest) and astrophysical simulations (e.g., MP-Gadget).
